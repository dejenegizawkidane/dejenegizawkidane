Methodology

  • First, identify the causality using the weak exogeneity test.
  • Then, based on the exogenetiy test, estimates the long-run relationship as:

\[ ln(price) = \beta_1 + \beta_2 ln(rev) + \mu \]

  • To check for cointegration we can estimate the Engle-Granger Cointegration model

\[ \Delta \mu_t = \rho \mu_{t-1} +\sum_{i=1}^p \gamma_i \Delta\mu_{t-i} +v_t \]

where $\mu_t$ is the estimated regression residuals extracted from the long-run model above.

  • The Engle-Granger model has some draw back as it did not account asymmetry relationship. To circumvent the problem, the threshold cointegration model is estimated:

\[ \Delta \mu_t = I_t\rho_1 \mu_{t-1}+(1-I_t)\rho_2 \mu_{t-1} +\sum_{i=1}^p \gamma_i \Delta\mu_{t-i} +v_t \]

where

\[\begin{equation} I_t=% \begin{cases} 1 &\text{if $\mu_{t-1}\geq \tau$ } \\ 0 &\text{if $\mu_{t-1} < \tau$} \end{cases} \end{equation}\]

or

\[\begin{equation} I_t=% \begin{cases} 1 &\text{if $\Delta \mu_{t-1}\geq \tau$ } \\ 0 &\text{if $\Delta \mu_{t-1} < \tau$} \end{cases} \end{equation}\]

Preliminary results from the models:

  • In each of the four major cities, I conducted threshold model estimations, exploring both zero threshold (\(\tau =0\) ) and nonzero threshold (\(\tau =! 0\) ) values. This entailed estimating four distinct models in each city, and I employed the AIC criteria to select the most suitable model.

  • Regarding the stationarity test, it’s worth noting that cointegration tests typically assess long-run relationships among non-stationary time series variables of order one. However, in some of the cities, one or even two variables exhibit stationarity at the level. However, this issue may potentially be addressed with the acquisition of the complete dataset.

  • Based on the weak exogeneity test, I took rev (denote revenue = inntekt/tota booked rom ) as explanatory variables, while hotel room price is dependent variable. So, in the tables below, $x_{pos}$ denotes positive deviations of rev and $x_{neg}$ denotes negative deviations of rev.

  • Conditions of convergence (\(\rho_1 < 0,\rho_2 < 0, and (1+\rho_1)(1+\rho_2) < 1\) ) are satisfied in all estimated models.

  • Co-integrating relationship is detected in all cases.

  • Asymmetry cointegration is detected only in the case of Oslo. However,I see some kind of consistency in the results, except in the case of Tromsø, as c-MTAR model is chosen in most of the cases, and the negative deviations adjust quickly. In the case of Tromsø, positive deviations adjust quickly.

  • The estimated speed of adjustments in most cases are greater than one (?).
